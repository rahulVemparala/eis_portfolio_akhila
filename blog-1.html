DESIGNING AI FOR ALL: A PRIMER ON BIAS IN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEMS

This is a comprehensive analysis of algorithmic bias as well as potential solutions to address it. It focuses on how the challenges of Algorithmic bias can be faced to provide fairness and accuracy in AI-supported decision-making. It highlights the significance of incorporating diversity in the design and decision-making phases to mitigate the risk of unintended biases. I believe, Bias can be encoded in subtle ways within the data or the algorithms themselves, and identifying and correcting for it requires a nuanced approach that takes into account the context in which the algorithm is being used. In my opinion, the best way to reduce algorithmic bias is to focus on increasing diversity and representation in the development of AI systems. This means involving people from a wide range of backgrounds and experiences in the design, development, and testing of algorithms. By doing so, we can ensure that a broader range of perspectives and voices are represented in the process and that potential biases are identified and addressed early on. I aspire to be a Data scientist as they can create unbiased models and ethical frameworks to ensure the recommendations are transparent and inclusive. For example- to avoid discriminatory and unnecessary product recommendations on shopping websites which could create complications for businesses and customers, data scientists must ensure compliance with regulatory standards and develop less biased algorithms.
